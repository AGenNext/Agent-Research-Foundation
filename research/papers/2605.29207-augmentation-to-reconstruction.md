## Question this paper answers

Why has the expected AI disruption not yet fully arrived, and what conditions determine
whether it produces broad welfare gains versus hardening today's market structures?

## Core thesis

The bottleneck on AI's impact is **not** model capability or tooling — it is that most
organizations still apply AI to workflows designed for a pre-AI world. The largest gains
require redesigning tasks, tools, and environments around AI's distinctive strengths.
This mirrors the historical productivity J-curve of general-purpose technologies (GPTs),
e.g. factory electrification, where gains surged only after layouts and processes were
redesigned around the new technology rather than substituted into old ones.

## Three-stage framework

These stages are **conceptual, not chronological** — an organization can occupy several
at once. Only Stage 3 produces genuine structural transformation. The contribution over
the standard GPT J-curve framing is characterizing the *organizational transitions*
(task-level enhancement → workflow redesign) that produce the aggregate pattern.

| Stage | Name | Description | Locus of change |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | **Augmentation** | AI enhances discrete human tasks inside existing workflows (drafting, summarizing, code suggestions). Humans keep sequencing, judgment, accountability. Spreads fast; gains are real but **local** — bottlenecks shift rather than disappear. | Task speed |
| 2 | **Automation** | Routine tasks move "under the hood" with less human awareness. Feels like the first real step-change, but runs inside human-era architectures (forms, queues, approvals, meetings). Heavy **intangible** complementary investment; observed productivity may understate progress. A **transitional, suboptimal equilibrium** — not a stable endpoint. | System runs harder |
| 3 | **Reconstruction** | Workflows redesigned around AI strengths: speed, parallelism, memory, continuous monitoring, machine-to-machine interaction. Steps re-sequenced/eliminated or converted into **auditable constraints**; governance engineered in, not bolted on. The activity itself is redefined ("shop", "learn", "code"). | System redefined |

### Domain progression (Table 1 of the paper)

| Domain | Augmentation | Automation | Reconstruction |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Shopping (B2C) | Draft lists, suggest products, summarize reviews | Delegate defined tasks ("reorder groceries", "flight < $500") | Personal agent continuously manages consumption; agent-to-agent negotiation; markets shift from browsing to discovery + quality assurance |
| Education (B2E) | Help complete assignments, explain concepts, draft lessons | Generate lesson plans, assessments, grading; real-time adaptive tutors | Continuous personalized learning loops; mastery tracking; emphasis shifts from static outputs to dynamic skill acquisition |
| News | Summarize, highlight, aggregate headlines | Personalized briefings filtered on preferences/behavior | Interactive, continuously updated dialogue; agents monitor sources, evaluate credibility, synthesize narratives; unit shifts from articles to agent-curated understanding |
| Coding (B2B) | Snippets, debugging, explaining functions | Automate pipeline segments (generation, testing, refactoring, docs) | Agents plan/generate/iterate end-to-end; humans move to problem formulation, architecture, curation; agent-to-agent execution |

> Coding is the domain furthest along into Stage 3.

## Why Stage 3 hasn't arrived: four institutional constraints

The binding constraints are **institutional, not technical**:

1. **Trust and accountability remain unresolved at scale** — delegation needs
   auditability, constraint enforcement, clear responsibility, incident response, and
   liability frameworks. Until trust infrastructure is routine, firms keep agents in
   assistive/advisory roles.
2. **Data and interfaces lag agentic needs** — reliable execution requires clean,
   interoperable data and explicit machine-readable policy. Weak interfaces and brittle
   pipelines raise error rates and compound risk.
3. **Workflows remain human-centric** — enterprise systems assume a human mediates every
   interaction (screens, forms, exceptions). Agent-first systems need APIs, protocols, and
   machine-readable rules — a structural redesign, not an added layer.
4. **Incentives favor optimization over reinvention** — optimizing within existing models
   is safer than rebuilding them. Incumbents can afford new infrastructure but lack
   incentive to disrupt themselves; entrants have incentive but limited capacity.

**Why Stage 2 won't last:** Stage 2 equilibria are stable only absent competitive
pressure. Once any actor demonstrates a reconstructed workflow, the calculus shifts fast;
prolonged Stage 2 also accrues organizational debt that makes later reconstruction costlier.
"The question is not whether reconstruction will occur, but whether incumbents will lead
it or be disrupted by it."

## Normative argument — "the Good Place"

- Default incentives risk **walled gardens**: agents in proprietary ecosystems that
  preserve friction, lock in users, and concentrate surplus.
- The future is **not predetermined**: leaders must "skate to where the puck is going"
  *and* actively steer it toward open, auditable, transformative designs before lock-in
  becomes irreversible.

### Actionable recommendations (from the paper)

- Invest in machine-readable data and interfaces that enable agent-to-agent interaction.
- Redesign workflows to be AI-native rather than layering automation onto existing processes.
- Define clear delegation boundaries and accountability structures for agent-driven decisions.

## Key terms

- **Productivity J-curve** — delayed, then surging, productivity from GPTs as intangible
  complementary investments mature (Brynjolfsson, Rock & Syverson 2021).
- **Agentic market** — a market mechanism where consumer agents and firm service agents
  coordinate, negotiate, and transact directly, with lower communication friction.
- **Auditable constraint** — a workflow rule encoded so agent behavior can be inspected
  and enforced rather than mediated by a human checkpoint.
